ABOUT

My name is Cole Speed, and I'm currently a Ph.D. student in the Department of Geological Sciences and a graduate researcher in the Quantitative Clastics Laboratory (QCL) at the University of Texas at Austin under the co-supervision of Zoltan Sylvester and David Mohrig. RESEARCH INTERESTS

My Ph.D. research seeks to quantify linkages between the morphologic and topographic evolution of planetary landscapes and their preservation in the rock record. Currently, I'm focusing on establishing these linkages within modern fluvial landscapes on Earth and the ancient rock record on Earth and Mars. I approach this problem through the integration of high-resolution topography, time-lapse satellite imagery, simple kinematic models, and field data. Ultimately, my work aims to predict both the evolution of rivers and the architecture of their resultant deposits, which has applications both in infrastructure management and in energy exploration / carbon sequestration.

My interests are continually evolving, and in the last few years I have started to dive much deeper into coding and the development of python-based workflows for processing and analyzing high-resolution imagery and topography. I've been fortunate for the opportunity to TA several courses in GIS/GPS and remote sensing methods, which has allowed me to further explore these interests.

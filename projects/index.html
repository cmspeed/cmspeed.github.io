Research

My primary interests are centered around two broad questions: How do planetary landscapes evolve? And how is this evolution preserved and reflected in the topographic and stratigraphic record? The lionshare of my research to-date focuses on fluvial landscapes, and more specifically on meandering river processes and depositional architecture.

THE EVOLUTION OF MEANDERING FLUVIAL LANDSCAPES

My current research focuses on characterizing the evolution of modern fluvial landscapes and their preservation in the ancient rock record using high-resolution topography, satellite imagery, stratigraphic models, and field geologic techniques. Ultimately, my work aims to predict both the evolution of rivers and the architecture of their deposits, which has applications in energy exploration and development and carbon sequestration.

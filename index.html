ABOUT

I am a Postdoctoral Fellow in the Radar Science Group (334H) within the Radar Science and Engineering Section at JPL. As part of the Observational Products for End-Users from Remote Sensing Analysis (OPERA) Project Science Team, my research focuses on quantifying the impacts of natural hazards, including earthquakes and subsidence-induced coastal flooding, through application of SAR- and optically-derived OPERA datasets (DSWx and DISP) and new sensor-agnostic ARIA products. A primary aim of my research is to aid in the mitigation of natural hazard impacts and disaster response and recovery efforts through application of satellite remote sensing data and approaches.

I obtained my Ph.D. in Geological Sciences at the University of Texas at Austin, where my dissertation focused on developing and applying techniques for automated monitoring of river dynamics using time-lapse optical satellite imagery, lidar-derived topography, and numerical modeling. Prior to my Ph.D. studies, I earned a B.Sc. in Geophysics from the University of Texas at Austin, where I conducted research applying active source seismology to image and reconstruct sea level-related coastal landscape dynamics within the shallow subsurface of the Gulf of Mexico. Over the last several years, I've worked to develop Python-based workflows for processing, analyzing, and visualizing geospatial datasets for my own use and that of the broader scientific community. I have deep interests in satellite remote sensing and open source geospatial software development and have collaborated with organizations including OpenTopography and JPL to develop tools for enhancing access and utilization of cloud-hosted resources.

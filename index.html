ABOUT

I am Ph.D. Candidate in the Department of Geological Sciences and a graduate researcher in the Quantitative Clastics Laboratory (QCL) at the University of Texas at Austin, Bureau of Economic Geology.

I am interested in problems spanning the fields of remote sensing, geospatial data analytics, and planetary surface processes. My Ph.D. research seeks to quantify linkages between the morphologic and topographic evolution of planetary landscapes and their preservation in the rock record. Currently, I'm focusing on establishing these linkages within modern fluvial landscapes on Earth and the ancient rock record on Earth and Mars. I approach this problem through the integration of high-resolution topography, time-lapse satellite imagery, simple kinematic models, and field data. Ultimately, my work aims to predict both the evolution of rivers and the architecture of their deposits, which has applications in infrastructure planning and energy exploration / carbon sequestration. Over the last few years, I have started to dive deeper into the development of Python workflows for processing, analyzing, and visualizing high-resolution imagery and topography and have recently started working on problems related to image segmentation and classification.
